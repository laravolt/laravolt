## 2024-05-01 - Collection `contains` vs `where()->first()` for Authorization Check
**Learning:** Using `contains()` on an Eloquent Collection or relation is significantly faster than using `where(...)->first()` followed by checking for existence or manually looping. In `where(...)->first()`, Laravel filters the *entire* collection creating a new collection of all matching elements before picking the first, whereas `contains()` short-circuits as soon as a match is found. For models, `contains($model)` also automatically delegates to `is()` checking under the hood and avoids explicit loops.
**Action:** Always prefer `contains()` or `containsStrict()` on Collections when checking for existence instead of `where(...)->first()` or `firstWhere()`, especially in high-frequency access patterns like role or permission checks.
